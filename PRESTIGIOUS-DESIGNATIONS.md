## üåç Calakmul UNESCO World Heritage Site

### UNESCO Recognition
- **Designated:** 2002 (Cultural Heritage)
- **Extended:** 2014 (Mixed Cultural & Natural Heritage)
- **Category:** Mixed World Heritage Site
- **Authority:** UNESCO (United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization)

### Site Significance

#### **Ancient Maya City**
- One of the **most important Maya archaeological sites** in Mesoamerica
- Rival to Tikal (Guatemala) in historical significance
- Over 6,750 ancient structures discovered
- Two massive pyramids (Structure I: 55m height, Structure II: 50m height)
- Population peak: 50,000+ inhabitants (600-900 AD)
- Political and ceremonial center of the Kaan Kingdom

#### **Protected Tropical Forests**
- **722,897 hectares** of protected biosphere reserve
- Second-largest tropical forest reserve in Americas (after Amazon)
- Part of the Mesoamerican Biological Corridor
- 86% forest coverage (one of the best-preserved in region)

#### **Biodiversity Hotspot**
- **350+ bird species** (including scarlet macaws, ocellated turkeys)
- **5 species of big cats:** Jaguars, pumas, ocelots, margays, jaguarundis
- Largest jaguar population in Mexico (400+ individuals)
- Central American tapirs, howler monkeys, spider monkeys
- Over 73 species of orchids
- 380+ butterfly species

### Distance from MayaRoyale
- **Approximately 200 km (124 miles)** from Isla Aguada
- **2.5-3 hour drive** via modern highways
- Perfect day-trip destination for resort guests
- Easily integrated into resort activity programming
